AccessScore is an analysis that assesses the infrastructure and demographic characteristics around transit stations that relate to how supportive of bicycling and walking the area is and how much bicycle and pedestrian activity could be occurring there. The analysis includes Regional Rail, light rail, trolley, and subway stations throughout the Philadelphia region with service provided by SEPTA, NJ TRANSIT, and PATCO.


To create AccessScore, DVRPC gathered and analyzed data on station area characteristics such as land use, demographics, transit density, safety, and non-motorized access. Characteristics within 2 miles of the station are scored on a scale of 1-5 and added together to create an overall AccessScore, meant to show how generally accessible a transit station is by all modes. Together, the scores of these individual characteristics, and a station's total AccessScore, will give transit agencies, trail advocacy groups and municipalities a picture of the station area characteristics that contribute to demand for bicycle and pedestrian facilities. Ultimately, AccessScore may help communities identify and prioritize improvements designed to strengthen pedestrian, cycling, and transit connectivity.


In addition to AccessScore, staff defined and calculated a PedestrianScore and a CycleScore for each station. Analysis definitions can be seen in the map to the right and here.

AccessScore

This analysis tool assesses the infrastructure and demographic characteristics around transit stations that relate to how supportive of bicycling and walking the area is and how much bicycle and pedestrian activity there is or could be. The analysis includes Region Rail and heavy rail, light rail, trolley, subway stations throughout the Philadelphia region with service provided by SEPTA, NJ TRANSIT, and PATCO.


To create this score, DVRPC staff collected data on station area characteristics that are likely to be factors in whether walk, cycle, or drive to transit stations. The sum of those factors, within two miles of the station, is calculated to give each station a total AccessScore. Together, these individual factors, and a station's total AccessScore, will give transit agencies, trail advocacy groups and municipalities a picture of the station area characteristics that contribute to demand for bicycle facilities and amenities. Ultimately, AccessScore may help communities identify and prioritize improvements designed to strengthen pedestrian, cycling, and transit connectivity.


In addition to AccessScore, which measures the potential demand for and supply of facilities for both walkers and people biking, the project team developed a score focused on pedestrians and one focused solely on bicycle-friendliness.


PedestrianScore: uses the existing sidewalk network to create a boundary for scoring walkability near stations. It defines the catchment area as the distance that can be walked continuously on existing sidewalks up to 1 mile. All categories are scored by what is present within that catchment area.


CycleScore: uses DVRPC's Bicycle Level of Traffic Stress (LTS) analysis to create a boundary for scoring cycling near stations. It defines the catchment areas as the distance that can be cycled continuously on LTS 1 and 2 road segments that abut the station up to two miles. All categories are scored by what is present within that catchment area.

This difference between passenger boards and the number of parked cars can be used as an approximation of the number of passengers who access the station via walking, bicycling or getting dropped off by someone else. Stations have been rated based on the percentage of nonparking passengers.

Scoring Analysis Methods

AccessScore: Uses the existing roadway network to create a boundary for scoring infrastructure and demographic characteristics for all users within two miles of a station.

PedestrianScore: Uses the existing sidewalk network to create a boundary for scoring walkability near stations. It defines the catchment area as the distance that can be walked continuously on existing sidewalks up to one mile.

CycleScore: Uses DVRPC’s Bicycle Level of Traffic Stress (LTS) analysis to create a boundary for scoring bicycle demand and access to stations. It defines the catchment areas as the distance that can be cycled continuously on LTS 1 (comfortable for most every cyclist) and 2 (comfortable for most adult riders) road segments that abut the station, up to two miles.
