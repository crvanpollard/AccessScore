This analysis tool assesses the infrastructure and demographic characteristics around transit stations that relate to how supportive of bicycling and walking the area is and how much bicycle and pedestrian activity there is or could be. The analysis includes Region Rail and heavy rail, light rail, trolley, subway stations throughout the Philadelphia region with service provided by SEPTA, NJ TRANSIT, and PATCO.


To create this score, DVRPC staff collected data on station area characteristics that are likely to be factors in whether walk, cycle, or drive to transit stations. The sum of those factors, within two miles of the station, is calculated to give each station a total AccessScore. Together, these individual factors, and a station's total AccessScore, will give transit agencies, trail advocacy groups and municipalities a picture of the station area characteristics that contribute to demand for bicycle facilities and amenities. Ultimately, AccessScore may help communities identify and prioritize improvements designed to strengthen pedestrian, cycling, and transit connectivity.

AccessScore

This analysis tool assesses the infrastructure and demographic characteristics around transit stations that relate to how supportive of bicycling and walking the area is and how much bicycle and pedestrian activity there is or could be. The analysis includes Region Rail and heavy rail, light rail, trolley, subway stations throughout the Philadelphia region with service provided by SEPTA, NJ TRANSIT, and PATCO.


To create this score, DVRPC staff collected data on station area characteristics that are likely to be factors in whether walk, cycle, or drive to transit stations. The sum of those factors, within two miles of the station, is calculated to give each station a total AccessScore. Together, these individual factors, and a station's total AccessScore, will give transit agencies, trail advocacy groups and municipalities a picture of the station area characteristics that contribute to demand for bicycle facilities and amenities. Ultimately, AccessScore may help communities identify and prioritize improvements designed to strengthen pedestrian, cycling, and transit connectivity.


In addition to AccessScore, which measures the demand for and supply of facilities for both walkers and people biking, the project team developed a score focused on pedestrians and one focused solely on bicycle-friendliness.


Pedestrian Score: uses the existing sidewalk network to create a boundary for scoring walkability near stations. It defines the catchment area as the distance that can be walked continuously on existing sidewalks up to 1 mile. All categories are scored by what is present within that catchment area.


Cycle Score: uses DVRPC’s Bicycle Level of Traffic Stress (LTS) analysis to create a boundary for scoring cycling near stations. It defines the catchment areas as the distance that can be cycled continuously on LTS 1 and 2 road segments that abut the station up to two miles. All categories are scored by what is present within that catchment area.

Station Area Land Use and Density

Civic and Cultural Attractors - This station characteristic aggregates the number of civic and cultural resources within 2 mile of the station. Civic and cultural resources aggregated include: libraries, US Post Offices, municipal buildings (excluding maintenance facilities), schools, colleges, museums, art galleries, botanical gardens, zoos.

    Scoring (number of resources)
  • High (5) = 27 - 93
  • Medium-High (4) = 19 - 26
  • Medium (3) = 12 - 18
  • Low-Medium (2) = 7 - 11
  • Low (1) = 1 - 6

Sources: NETS database (2015), National Center for Education Statistics (NCES), DVRPC.


Employees - This station characteristic measures the number of known employees, as identified in National Establishment Time-Series (NETS) data, within 2-mile of the station. Employees located near transit may be more likely to use transit; with incentives like proximity, lack of parking, and quality sidewalk and trail connections, that likelihood could increase as the density of employees increases near the station.

    Scoring: Total employees within 2-mile of the station (quantiles).
  • High (5) = 53,363 - 366,592
  • Medium-High (4) = 34,064 - 53,362
  • Medium (3) = 28,328 - 34,063
  • Low-Medium (2) = 22,945 - 28,327
  • Low (1) = 3,368 - 22,944

Sources: NETS database (2015), DVRPC.


Essential Services - Places of employment, grocery stores, schools, medical care facilities, recreation/open space, senior centers, and centers for the developmentally disabled.

    Scoring: Total number of essential services within 2 mile of the station (quantiles).
  • High (5) = 264 - 480
  • Medium-High (4) = 105 - 263
  • Medium (3) = 60 - 104
  • Low-Medium (2) = 36 - 59
  • Low (1) = 6 - 35
To learn more about essential services throughout the DVRPC region visit our Equity Through Access Maptoolkit.

Sources: DVRPC (Circuit Trails, 2020; Parks/Open Space, 2016), HRSA (2020), NCES (2017-2018), NETS (2015).


Parks and Open Space - Transit stations have been evaluated for their proximity to outdoor destinations in the Delaware Valley that may attract cyclists and walkers. These destinations include federal, state, and county parks (greater than 10 acres), arboretums, and cemeteries. Scores measure a station's proximity to any of these types of recreational bicycle and pedestrian destinations.

    Scoring:
  • High (5) = On/adjacent (within ¼ mile)
  • Medium-High (4) = ¼-1/2 mile
  • Medium (3) = 1/2 mile - 1 mile
  • Low-Medium (2)= 1 - 2 miles
  • Low (1) = > 2 miles

Sources: DVRPC, with feedback from the Bicycle Coalition of Greater Philadelphia.


Walkable Retail Corridors and Centers - This measure evaluates a transit station’s proximity to a concentration of walkable retail uses. In Philadelphia County, the Philadelphia Planning Commission has identified "Walkable Commercial Corridors" - specific portions of corridors that are both comfortable for a pedestrian and have a retail focus. Within the surrounding counties, DVRPC has identified "Centers" - places with a unique sense of place, existing infrastructure, and opportunities for development. Taken together, these corridors and places make up some of the region's most notable destinations.

    Scoring:
  • High (5) = On/adjacent (within ¼ mile)
  • Medium-High (4) = ¼-1/2 mile
  • Medium (3) = 1/2 mile - 1 mile
  • Low-Medium (2)= 1 - 2 miles
  • Low (1) = > 2 miles

Source: Philadelphia Department of Planning and Development, DVRPC.

Demographics

Indicators of Potential Disadvantage - The Indicators of Potential Disadvantage (IPD) analysis identifies populations of interest under Title VI and EJ using U.S. Census American Community Survey (ACS) 2015-2019 five-year estimates data and maps these populations in each of the Census tracts in the region via GIS. Each population group is an “indicator” in the analysis and includes the following (Youth, Older Adults, Female, Racial Minority, Ethnic Minority, Foreign-Born, Limited English Proficiency, Disabled, Low-Income)

    Scoring based of IPD Overall Score
  • High (5) = well above average
  • Medium-High (4) = above average
  • Medium (3) = average
  • Low-Medium (2) = below average
  • Low (1) = well below average
To learn more visit:Equity Analysis for the Greater Philadelphia Region.

Sources: U.S. Census Bureau (2019), DVRPC.


Population Density - Transit stations are rated on the concentration of residents - potential transit customers and origins for transit - within 1-mile of the station. Greater population densities within station areas increase the potential for a transit customer base.

    Scoring: total persons within 2-miles of the station
  • High (5) = 248,250 - 329,445
  • Medium-High (4) = 155,876 - 248,249
  • Medium (3) = 92,875 - 155,875
  • Low-Medium (2) = 67,778 - 92,874
  • Low (1) = 29,802 - 67,777

Source: U.S. Census Bureau (2019).


Zero Vehicle Households - Zero Vehicle Households are families or individuals that do not own or have access to a vehicle and use transit, walking, non-motorized modes, or ridesharing to make trips. Zero Vehicle Households uses tract-level ASC 2019.

    Scoring: total number of zero vehicle households within 2-miles of the station
  • High (5) = 6,824 - 11,700
  • Medium-High (4) = 4,286 - 6,823
  • Medium (3) = 2,274 - 4,285
  • Low-Medium (2) = 1,502 - 2,273
  • Low (1) = 602 - 1,501

Source: U.S. Census Bureau (2019)

Transit Density and Access

Non-Parking Passenger Boards -Transit stations are evaluated for the number of vehicles parked at the station as a percentage of the number of passengers that board trains. This difference between passenger boards and the number of parked cars can be used as an approximation of the number of passengers who access the station via walking, bicycling or getting dropped off by someone else. Stations have been rated based on the percentage of non-parking passengers.

    Scoring (percent of non-parking passengers)
  • High (5) = 94.5% - 100%
  • Medium-High (4) = 76.83% - 94.49%
  • Medium (3) = 63.59% - 76.82%
  • Low-Medium (2) = 49.37% - 63.58%
  • Low (1) = 0% - 49.36%

Sources: SEPTA, NJTRANSIT, PATCO, DVRPC.


Percentage of Local Drivers - This layer uses DVRPC’s most recent license plate survey data for each station to determine the percentage of vehicles driving to and parking at a station from within a two mile radius. For more information on how license plate survey data is collected, please visit DVRPC’s Park and Ride Passenger Origins Webmap.

    Scoring: total vehicles from 2 miles or less / total of count cars from survey year (quantilies)
  • High (5) = 0.79% - 1%
  • Medium-High (4) = 0.62% - 0.78%
  • Medium (3) = 0.43% - 0.61%
  • Low-Medium (2) = 0.3% - 0.42%
  • Low (1) = 0.0% - 0.29%

Source: DVRPC (2020).


(coming soon....) Transit Vehicle Volume - Transit Vehicle Volume is the total number of buses and trains that serve the area at or around each station per day. The larger the volume of transit vehicles, the more connected a place is, as it's the concentrations of transit vehicles - both rail and bus - that create options for riders to access destinations via public transit. Each station is scored on how many transit vehicles pass within 500 feet of the station in a twenty-four hour time period.

    Scoring: total transit vehicles within 24 hours

Source: SEPTA, NJTRANSIT, PATCO, Google, DVRPC.

Bicycle and Pedestrian Safety and Access

Circuit Trail Proximity - This measure assesses how close a transit station is to a Circuit Trail, Greater Philadelphia’s existing and planned network of 750 miles of walking and biking trails aimed at connecting the region to communities, jobs, and parks. Sections of the network are at various stages of the design and construction process. Programmed sections, those sections that have secured funding or are under construction, and existing sections, those sections that have been built and are open to the public, were included in this measure.

    Scoring:
  • High (5) = On/adjacent (within ¼ mile)
  • Medium-High (4) = ¼-1/2 mile
  • Medium (3) = 1/2 mile - 1 mile
  • Low-Medium (2)= 1 - 2 miles
  • Low (1) = > 2 miles

Source: DVRPC.


Connectivity Score - A Connectivity Score is a rating of the connectivity of a place's street network that can be used to determine a community’s walkability. Connectivity scores for stations are based on the intersection density within 2-mile of the station.

    Scoring: intersection density within 2-mile of station
  • High (5) = 0.87 - 1.49
  • Medium-High (4) = 0.55 - 0.87
  • Medium (3) = 0.35 - 0.54
  • Low-Medium (2) = 0.21 - 0.34
  • Low (1) = 0.04 - 0.20

Source: DVRPC.


Crashes - Crashes reports and sums the number of reportable crashes within two miles of the station. Total crashes are then scored from low (1) to high (5). The number of crashes that resulted in death or a severe injury generally, and specifically for a pedestrian or a bicyclist are labeled.

    Scoring: 2015-2019 KSI Crashes within 2 miles of station
  • High (5)= 1,255 - 2,083
  • Medium-High (4) = 548 - 1,254
  • Medium (3) = 379 - 547
  • Low-Medium (2) = 265 - 378
  • Low (1) = 65 - 264
To learn more visit:DVRPC Crash Data Viewer.

Sources: PennDOT, NJDOT.


Low Stress Bicycle Shed - This station characteristic shows the connected roadway segments (also known as LTS islands) that abut the station and fall into Level of Traffic Stress 1 and 2. Segments that are LTS 1 and 2 are considered to be suitable for bicycling for people of all ages and abilities. The score for this category is determined by the sum length in lineal feet of the LTS 1+2 islands are placed into 5 categories and ranked shortest to longest.

    Scoring: LTS Low-stress (1+2) islands sum of length in miles (quantilies)
  • High (5)= 206.85 - 336.56
  • Medium-High (4) = 122.41 - 206.84
  • Medium (3) = 43.53 - 122.40
  • Low-Medium (2) = 13.03 - 43.52
  • Low (1) = 0 - 13.02

Source: DVRPC.


Sidewalk Network Shed - This station characteristic shows sidewalk networks that abut stations. The score for this category is determined by the sum length in lineal feet of the sidewalks (up to one mile). These sums are placed into 5 categories and ranked shortest to longest.

    Scoring:
  • High (5)= 536.8 - 2337.84
  • Medium-High (4) = 267.49 - 536.79
  • Medium (3) = 106.27 - 267.48
  • Low-Medium (2) = 22.58 - 106.26
  • Low (1) = 0 - 22.57

Source: DVRPC.
